Team Roping Events in Arizona

Arizona is one of the premier team roping states in the western United States, offering what very few other markets can match: year-round competitive roping in a desert climate that makes outdoor events feasible from September through May, and sometimes all twelve months at facilities with shade and lighting. The state's strong snowbird population swells the roping community significantly from November through March, when competitors from California, Nevada, Colorado, and the Midwest haul horses to Arizona to escape winter and compete in the desert sun.

The core of Arizona roping is anchored by a string of competitive communities running through the Phoenix metro area and beyond. Wickenburg hosts the state's highest volume of events, anchored by Rancho Rio. The West Valley markets of Wittmann and Buckeye are served by 4D Arena and surrounding facilities, while the central corridor includes Maricopa and Casa Grande. East of Phoenix, Queen Creek and the Gilbert area offer additional competitive venues. Prescott and the Yavapai County corridor add events in the central highlands.

Arizona events draw quality competitors — the state's roping community includes numerous former and current PRCA contestants, USTRC national event qualifiers, and serious amateurs who compete heavily across the #10 through open divisions. The cattle supply is strong through partnerships with Arizona and New Mexico ranching operations. Entry fees across Arizona events run $100 to $350 per man, with events ranging from small weeknight jackpots to major productions with added money.
