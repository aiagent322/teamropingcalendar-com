Team Roping Events in Benton City, Washington

Benton City, Washington is the center of the Columbia Basin team roping circuit and the most active roping community in the Pacific Northwest outside of Spokane. The agricultural heartland of eastern Washington supports a strong roping culture built on ranching, rodeo, and a deep connection to western competitive sports. Benton City arena facilities host some of the most competitive jackpots in the Pacific Northwest, drawing competitors from throughout Washington, Oregon, Idaho, and occasionally Montana. Events here run primarily in spring through fall, taking advantage of the high desert climate of the Columbia Basin. Entry fees at Benton City events typically run $120 to $250 per man, with competitive fields that include ropers who compete seriously at regional and national levels. The proximity to the Tri-Cities area — Kennewick, Pasco, and Richland — gives Benton City access to one of the largest population centers in eastern Washington, contributing to strong entry numbers at regular jackpots.
