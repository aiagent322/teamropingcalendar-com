Team Roping Events in Hobbs, New Mexico

Hobbs, New Mexico is the team roping hub of the Permian Basin's New Mexico side, drawing competitors from Carlsbad, Lovington, Artesia, and across southeastern New Mexico and West Texas. Lea County's strong oil and agricultural economy supports an active roping community with consistent jackpot activity at local arena facilities year-round. Hobbs ropers benefit from proximity to the Texas Permian Basin market — Midland and Odessa are less than two hours east — creating a competitive pool that draws from both New Mexico and West Texas. Events in Hobbs range from small community jackpots in the lower number draws to competitive open ropings that attract serious regional competitors. Entry fees typically run $100 to $250 per man. New Mexico's Corriente cattle supply supports quality event production, and Hobbs producers are known for running organized, consistent ropings.
